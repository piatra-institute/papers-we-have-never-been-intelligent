# Research

Findings, tiered by source proximity.

## Findings

- [T1] Modernity works by purification (a declared nature/society split) running alongside the proliferation of hybrids the split forbids; the more the moderns purify, the more hybrids they make — Latour, *We Have Never Been Modern* (1993). The structural template the paper transposes onto intelligence (§2).
- [T1] The laboratory builds a hybrid and then strips the network off it so the inscription can leave as pure fact — Latour and Woolgar, *Laboratory Life* (1986); Latour, *Science in Action* (1987). Source of the purification/inscription mechanism reused as attributional compression (§2, §6).
- [T1] Otto's notebook argument: under coupling conditions an external resource is part of the cognitive system — Clark and Chalmers (1998), *Analysis* 58(1):7–19. The hybrid at its smallest; the paper grants the critics and rests elsewhere (§3, §5).
- [T2] Scaffolding, not constitution: causal dependence on an environment built over a life need not make the environment part of the mind — Sterelny (2010), *Phenomenology and the Cognitive Sciences* 9(4):465–481; Adams and Aizawa (2008); Rupert (2004), *J. Philosophy* 101(8):389–428. Used to show the metaphysical debate is the wrong fight (§5).
- [T1] A cognitive system can span a team, instruments, and procedure and compute what no member computes alone (ship navigation; cockpit) — Hutchins, *Cognition in the Wild* (1995); Hollan, Hutchins and Kirsh (2000), *ACM ToCHI* 7(2):174–196. The realization-exceeds-individual claim the paper leans on (§5, §6).
- [T2] Humans' specialism is enrollability into cumulative culture via high-fidelity social learning and shared intentionality, not raw individual brainpower — Tomasello (1999); Tomasello et al. (2005), *BBS* 28(5):675–691; Henrich (2016); Heyes (2018); Boyd and Richerson (1985); Donald (1991). Grounds the capacity/historical-possibility split and the biological objection answered by relocation (§4, §8).
- [T1] Notation is cognitive machinery, not a record of thought: the Greek lettered diagram makes deduction possible — Netz (1999); symbolic writing is a technology for the otherwise unthinkable — Rotman (2000). The mathematics case (§6).
- [T2] Trained scientific perception ("the scientific self") is an institutional product drilled over years — Daston and Galison, *Objectivity* (2007); epistemic cultures make knowledge through machinery that disappears from the result — Knorr Cetina (1999); Galison (1997). The laboratory case and the purification of perception (§6).
- [T2] Scientific credit resolves collective production into named authorship through a social credit economy — Shapin, *A Social History of Truth* (1994). The settlement/credit-economy argument (§7).
- [T2] The individual unit of intelligence is a deliberate historical construction tied to merit, testing, and the meritocratic state — Gould, *The Mismeasure of Man* (1981); Carson, *The Measure of Merit* (2007); Danziger, *Constructing the Subject* (1990). Grounds the load-bearing-fiction / closed-book-exam genealogy and the psychometric objection answered by relocation (§7, §8).
- [T2] Tacit knowledge is the unstatable, participation-grown part of expertise — Polanyi (1966); Collins (2010); Lave and Wenger (1991); Dreyfus (1992). Redescribes agency/genius as skilled coupling (§8).
- [T3] "Cognitive assemblage" is N. K. Hayles's prior term for human-technical cognitive couplings — Hayles, *Unthought* (2017). Acknowledged so the paper's usage is not presented as novel (§3).
- [T4] AI as a corpus + compute + RLHF + tooling compressed into a noun (stochastic-parrots framing, situated machine action) — Suchman (2007); contemporary AI-critique discourse. Used only illustratively, claiming AI exposes rather than creates the attribution problem (§6, §8); no empirical AI claim is asserted.
