# Brief

## Question

Does the modern category of intelligence systematically misattribute performances generated by brains, bodies, symbols, artifacts, collaborators, and institutions to a supposedly self-sufficient individual mind, and can Latour's concept of constitutional purification explain that mislocation without denying biology, individual differences, or agency?

## Claim

Humans hold real, unequal cognitive capacities but have never been intelligent alone. A Cognitive Constitution depends operationally on cognitive assemblages while representationally and normatively purifying their outputs into properties of bounded individuals; the operation is attributional compression. The individual mind is the output of that compression, not its input, which is why it survives every demonstration that cognition is distributed: purification is not an error but the accounting system of merit, the way credit, credentials, wages, blame, and personhood get attached to a single body. Intelligence names a settlement about credit, not a measurement of where thinking happens.
